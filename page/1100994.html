oldest living confederate widow tells all

Authors: allan_gurganus

Publish Year: 1989

Publisher: alfred_a_knopf

Rating: 3.84

Description: an astonishing first novel about the civil war and its aftermath in our own time in the raucous voice of a woman who lived through it alllucy marsden is the wife of the civil wars last surviving soldier she is now halfblind 99 years old locked into a charity home and very talkative the darkly comic tale that lucy tells straddles two centuries and forgives neither her ribald blunt confession reveals two wars one declared in 1861 the other genderinspired domestic and eternal here are shermans fires the slaves local pain and mythic imaginations the mixed joys of motherhood and lucys own progress as the child bride of her crazed and earnest civil war vet her taletold epic offers a gallery of moral characters aristocrats and sharecroppers black and white slave and free lee and lincoln among them in her folk perspective the south is a realm where fact and fable are both truethis is a joyously told stubbornly ungrammatical last testament that offers a fond portrait of one plain womans unending battle one that is masterful and lasting
